HTML5 Transition

Published Sat, January 22, 2011
Tags: webmastering; html5; blog;

Recently, the HTML5 logo was published. As W3C states, [i]t stands strong and true, resilient and universal as the markup you write. […] And it certainly doesn’t use tables for layout. The logo’s site presents you with many information about HTML5, the new possibilities and the new logo. Due to that, I decided to make use of HTML5. Right now, the blog is using the HTML5 semantics (header, footer, nav, article figure, figcaption) and CSS3 (border-radius, box-shadow, text-shadow). The CSS3 part is still in development.

Update: The CSS3 part is ready.
